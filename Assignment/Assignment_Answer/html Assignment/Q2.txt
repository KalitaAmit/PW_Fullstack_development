Q2. What are semantic HTML elements? Provide examples of three semantic tags and explain their
significance.

Ans:-
 
Semantic HTML elements are tags that convey the meaning of their content to both the browser and developers. Unlike generic <div> and <span> elements, semantic tags clearly define the purpose of the content, improving readability, accessibility, and SEO.

Examples of Semantic Tags and Their Significance

(1)<header>

Purpose: Represents the introductory section of a page or a section, typically containing navigation links, logos, or headings.
Significance: Helps search engines and screen readers recognize the header as a key section, improving SEO and accessibility.

<header>
    <h1>My Website</h1>
    <nav>
        <a href="home.html">Home</a>
        <a href="about.html">About</a>
    </nav>
</header>



(2)<article>

Purpose: Represents a self-contained, independent content block, such as a blog post, news article, or forum post.
Significance: Helps search engines recognize the content as a distinct piece of information, improving indexing.

(3)<footer>

Purpose: Represents the footer section of a webpage or an article, typically containing copyright information, links, or contact details.
Significance: Helps in organizing page layout while improving accessibility and SEO.
